\begin{abstract}
\noindent \textbf{AND} personalized student timetabling is an important problem in universities, where students must select a schedule of courses that satisfies institutional constraints such as prerequisites, seat limits, and non-overlapping schedules, while also considering personal preferences, such as avoiding early classes, grouping courses into fewer days, or preferring certain professors. Previous work using backtracking has shown that it can be effective in smaller scenarios, including a real implementation used by pre-graduate students of FIIS (Facultad de Ingeniería Industrial y de Sistemas) at UNI (Universidad Nacional de Ingeniería), which demonstrated that the problem can be solved in practice and was used by hundreds of students every semester.

\noindent \textbf{BUT} as the number of courses, constraints, and possible combinations increases, classical approaches such as backtracking or linear programming start to scale poorly, and generating high-quality schedules can become too slow or impractical, especially in more complex casuistics. At the same time, many existing solutions do not fully explore recent advances in artificial intelligence for combinatorial optimization that could guide the search more efficiently.

\noindent \textbf{THEREFORE} this research proposes to investigate and design a scalable framework for personalized student timetabling, based on AI-augmented optimization techniques that combine classical search methods with approaches such as reinforcement learning and neural-guided heuristics, with the objective of improving scalability, solution quality, and adaptability to different institutional contexts, while also developing a web-based prototype that allows real-world usage and evaluation with students.
