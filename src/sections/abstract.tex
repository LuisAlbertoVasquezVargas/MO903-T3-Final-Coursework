\tightabstract{
\textbf{AND} the \PSTP is an important challenge in universities, where students must select course schedules that satisfy institutional constraints while considering personal preferences, such as avoiding early classes or preferring specific professors. Previous work has proven that backtracking can be effective for the \PSTP in smaller scenarios, including a real implementation used by students of the \FIIS at the \UNI in Peru, showing that the problem can be solved in practice for hundreds of students every semester.

\textbf{HOWEVER}, as the number of courses and constraints increases, classical approaches like backtracking or linear programming scale poorly, making high-quality schedule generation impractical in more complex scenarios. Furthermore, existing solutions often fail to explore recent advances in \AI for combinatorial optimization that could guide the search more efficiently.

\textbf{THEREFORE}, this research proposes a scalable framework for the \PSTP based on \AI-augmented optimization. By combining classical search with reinforcement learning and neural-guided heuristics, the system aims to improve scalability and adaptability across different institutional contexts. A web-based prototype will be developed to evaluate the framework's performance through real-world student usage.
