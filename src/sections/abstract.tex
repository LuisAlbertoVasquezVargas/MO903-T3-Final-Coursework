\begin{abstract}
This coursework explores the development of an AI-augmented framework for the Personalized Student Timetabling Problem (PSTP). While classical backtracking methods have proven effective in constrained environments—such as the implementation for FIIS at the Universidad Nacional de Ingeniería—they lack scalability for complex institutional contexts. This study analyzes a scalable architecture that integrates classical combinatorial optimization with neural-guided heuristics. The results suggest that augmenting traditional search methods with AI techniques significantly improves solution quality and adaptability, providing a robust foundation for real-world web-based scheduling prototypes.
\end{abstract}
