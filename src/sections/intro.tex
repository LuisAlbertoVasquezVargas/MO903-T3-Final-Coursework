\section{Introduction}
The Personalized Student Timetabling Problem (PSTP), a specialized variant of the Student Scheduling Problem (SSP), consists of assigning students to specific course sections while satisfying institutional hard constraints and individual soft preferences. Working on this problem is critical because, in institutions like the National University of Engineering (UNI) in Peru, the manual selection process is often described as tedious due to a massive combinatorial search space, frequently leading to sub-optimal schedules and high student dissatisfaction during enrollment.

The primary challenge lies in the NP-complete nature of the underlying scheduling problem, which is further complicated by the real-time saturation of sections. Most related works prioritize Educational Timetabling (ET), which focuses on institutional-level master schedules (faculty and classroom availability) but fails to address the individual convenience layer, such as avoiding gaps between classes or selecting preferred instructors.

This research contributes a novel hybrid framework that enhances classical backtracking, which was previously proven effective at a local scale in the FIIS-UNI implementation, with AI-augmented optimization. By integrating Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) to guide search heuristics, the proposed system aims to prune the search space more effectively for complex, large-scale scenarios. To validate the framework, we will develop a web-based prototype and evaluate its performance against standard backtracking and linear programming baselines using real-world academic data, focusing on runtime efficiency and solution quality.
